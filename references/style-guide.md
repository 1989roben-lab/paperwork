# Paperwork Style Guide

## Language

- Default to bilingual output: one Chinese paragraph followed by one English paragraph for each field.
- Do not label paragraphs with `中文`, `English`, `Chinese`, or similar language markers unless the user explicitly asks for labels.
- Keep Chinese direct and businesslike. Avoid ornate phrasing.
- Keep English natural, concise, and manager-ready. Avoid literal translation when a cleaner business phrase works better.
- Preserve the same meaning across Chinese and English.

## Tone

- Write for MongoDB internal business review unless the user says otherwise.
- Be specific about customer, workload, business impact, timeline, and assumptions.
- Avoid exaggerated claims, unverifiable promises, and vague adjectives.
- Separate user-provided facts from estimates or assumptions.
- Prefer confident but measured wording such as `assuming`, `expected`, and `recommended`; avoid `based on` or `estimate` wording when a specific paperwork reference asks for more natural customer-discovery language.
- Do not expose internal calculation steps, scratchpad reasoning, pricing multiplication, or evaluation process in final paperwork. Show only the recommendation, concise rationale, assumptions, and follow-up confirmations.

## Structure

- Use the user's field names exactly when provided.
- Keep answers short enough to paste into internal systems.
- Use clear field headings, then compact paragraphs instead of long bullet lists unless the field asks for steps.
- Put Chinese and English as separate natural paragraphs under each field heading, with no language labels.
- Bold important numbers, dates, and configuration names when it improves readability.
- For missing non-critical details, proceed with reasonable assumptions and mention them briefly.
- For missing critical details, ask the minimum necessary question before drafting.

## Quality Checklist

Before finalizing, check that:

- every required field is present,
- every field has Chinese and English,
- fields do not label the Chinese or English paragraphs,
- English is not a word-for-word translation of awkward Chinese,
- numbers, dates, customer names, workload names, and currencies are consistent,
- estimates are labeled as estimates,
- internal evaluation process is not included in final text,
- the output can be pasted directly into a company form.
